Gear in Splatoon

You are what you wear...

The world of Splatoon has quite the sense of style, and this is exemplified in its' character customization. Along with the ability to change gender, species, eye colour, hair style and pants style, Splatoon 2 has over 570 pieces of headgear, shirts and shoes.

While style is nice and all, gear serves a deeper purpose in Turf Wars: it enhances your abilities. Each piece of gear has a main ability, along with three up to sub-abilities. Some allow you to warp to teammates quicker, some to conserve or store more ink, some to increase steath or power in certain circumstances. The combinations are endless, and most players choose to select gear that fits their weapon and playstyle.

Tools of the Trade

Speaking of weapons and playstyle, here's a look of the different weapon classes Splatoon 2 offers. Click on the the thumbnail to see a demonstration of the weapon.

Weapon Class Description Example Weapon
Shooter A traditional gun-type weapon. Varying accuracy. Typically auto-fires, but some burst-shot models also exist.
Roller Large paint rollers. Ink a lot of turf holding to the ground. Can fling paint vertically or horizontally.
Brush Technically a roller subclass, but operates differently enough that it is often treated separate. Tracks quicker than a roller, and swings in quick arcs as opposed to flings.
Charger A precise-shot weapon that (usually) kills in one hit with a full charge. Analagous to a sniper rifle.
Slosher A bucket repurposed as a weapon, "sloshing" ink as a form of attack. Varying range. Some have special bullets, such as bubbles or explosions.
Splatling A charge-style weapon similar to Chargers, but produces a spray of ink droplets instead of one solid line.
Dualie Similar to the Shooter class, but comes in a pair of weapons. Allows the user to do a specified number of quick rolls while shooting, useful for quick maneuvering and dodging.
Brella Shoots short shots of ink, and can deploy a barrier in front of the user. Useful for tactful defense.
